It’s a web interface where farmers could directly interact with manufacturers to get right value for their produce by eliminating middlemen in the process

Project Overview


Agro Products To Industry is an endeavour in this direction to create one stop shop for meeting all informational needs relating to Agriculture, sale/storage of an Indian farmer.


With this Indian Farmer will not be required to sift through maze of websites created for specific purposes. Once in the Agro Products To Industry website, a farmer will be able to get all relevant information on specific subjects around his village/block /district or state and could contact the right manufacturer




Problem Context


Agricultural marketing still continues to be in a bad shape in rural India. Farmers, right from sowing the seeds to dispatching the grown crops inverst a lot of time.


Instead of their efforts being paid off, they are blind sided from their earnings because of the structure in place. As not every manufacturer gets the farm products at the “FACTORY PRICE”, they go through three to four middlemen and reach them with almost double the actual price.


In the absence of sound marketing facilities, the farmers have to depend upon local traders and middlemen for the disposal of their farm produce which is sold at throw-away price.




Solution


Our solution is involved in making a liable model to put an end to the trauma and difficulty faced by the farmers because of the middlemen.


Basically, this model will work for getting the details of the products from farmers/consumers. Get the right value of the verified product and uploading it in the application.

Design


The design comprises of two modules where manufacturer and farmer are the major users.


The process involved is in three steps

  • Research/ field work
  • Analysis
  • Final design

Research


After contacting many farmers in Rolugunta village in the state of Andhra Pradesh in India, we observed pain points like

  • Low produce value
  • Lack of education
  • Less communication with manufacturers


Analysis


Our analysis included in the comparison of price of a bag that farmers sells to the middlemen and the difference when it reaches to the manufacturer where he buys from the middlemen


We observed this with a bag of rice and pulses
For example :

  • 1 Quintal - 100 kg costs 1800 Rs, so 25 kg bag is around 450 Rs, the farmer sells its around 800 Rs per bag the middlemen. Finally, the manufacturer buys the 25 kg bag for 1250 Rs with a profit of 400 Rs for the middlemen.

  • In the pulses like Black-gram, Red-gram and Sesame seeds, the farmer sells around 60 Rs per kg and the manufacturer when he buys from middlemen it costs around 100-120 Rs per kg with a difference of 40-60 Rs


Final Design


After considering all the pain points we laid out the Information Architecture of the interface. Based on the Information Architecture we removed the unnecessary things.

The final design involved in the design of farmer module and manufacturer module




Information Architecture


Farmer Module


Farmer registers himself or somebody behalf of him in the webpage and logs into the portal He views the government policies, cultivation details in the webpage



Some of the major actions done in the portal are

  • Show manufacturers (generates manufacturers report)
  • Update profile
  • market price
  • Agreement rules
  • Transportation cost


Show manufactures generates the report of the listed manufacturers who are interested to buy the products in the region



Market price shows the difference of the prices that market has with the manufacturer buys from the middlemen



Agreement rules are the agreement between the farmer and manufacturer, in which both of them agree upon



Transportation cost webpage gives the estimation of transportation distance and if third party transport is required it redirects to another vendor named “TruckGuru” truckguru.co.in
Transportation distance is given by “Google maps” from point A to B

Conclusion

Even though the application helps in a large way with some of the limitations like


  • Every farmer may not be literate

    • So for each village there is an agricultural officer who can guide the farmer through the process and access will be given to the next literate in the farmer’s family


  • Transportation issues for the farmer

    • Every farmer sometimes don’t have enough money for direct transport, so manufacturer can provide complimentary transport facilities for the farmer.
